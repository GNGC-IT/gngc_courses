
# Information Technology - Evidence Guide

## Objectives

This assessment requires you to demonstrate your understanding of the design, development, and evaluation of technical systems. You must address the following criteria:

- Design Process & Methodologies: Explain the specific design methodology used (e.g., Engineering Design Cycle, Agile, Waterfall, Cisco PPDIOO) and evaluate how constraints (time, budget, hardware, user needs) influenced your decisions.
    
- Theoretical Application: Analyse the underlying theories, concepts, and principles relevant to your discipline (e.g., Circuit Theory, Network Protocols, Encryption Standards, Data Structures) and explain how they were applied to solve the specific problem.
    
- Critical Testing & Evaluation: Think critically to solve problems by drawing on data from testing. Document the testing process, analyse failures, and justify the solutions implemented.
    
- Impact & Ethics: Analyse the design’s impact on the user and environment. Consider and plan for ethical, sustainable, and accessible implications (e.g., WHS, E-waste, Data Privacy, User Accessibility).
    

## Introduction

A major part of any technical project is documentation. In industry, documentation ensures knowledge transfer, allowing systems to be maintained, scaled, and secured by others. In education, it is the primary evidence of your understanding. A working prototype proves you can do it; your documentation proves you understand how and why it works.

## Method

Your task is to create an artefact that documents your project's lifecycle. This artefact can be a single comprehensive document or a combination of media (e.g., a report linked to a video walkthrough or a website). Regardless of the format, it must provide evidence of the following core phases:

1. Ideation & Research
    
- What problem are you solving? (The Brief)
    
- What existing solutions or theories informed your approach? (Research)
    
- Why did you choose these specific tools/components? (Justification)
    

3. Design Methodologies
    

- How did you build it? Show the evolution of your solution.
    

5. Theoretical Application
    

- Where is the "Proof of Logic"? (See table below for subject-specific examples).
    

7. Testing & Refinement
    

- How do you know it works? Provide data, logs, or screenshots of successful tests.
    
- What went wrong? Document the bugs/issues and the specific steps taken to resolve them.
    

9. Evaluation & Impact
    

- Did you meet the brief? Evaluate your success against the original requirements.
    
- Is it safe and ethical? Provide a Risk Assessment, Security Audit, or Sustainability Check as appropriate.
    

## Subject-Specific Guidance

Use the table below to understand what "Proof of Logic" and "Testing" look like for your specific stream.

|Course|Proof of Logic (Theory)|Testing Evidence (Data)|
|---|---|---|
|Robotics & Mechatronics|Schematics & Flowcharts: Diagrams showing circuit connections (Ohm's law) and code logic flow.|Debug Log: Multimeter readings, serial monitor output, and photos of physical fixes.|
